Mr.Chaussette Battle Tournament

  • Genre: Versus Fighting
  • Mode: 2-player
  • Development Time: 4 weeks
  • Team: 8 people
  • Platform: PC

This project was a end-of-year project where we decided to explore a new genre we never designed: Versus Fighting.
It was a challenging and ambitious project, especially since I was the only Tech Designer.

Local Multiplayer

  • Two player controllers can be spawned to play with a second controller.
  • The camera is set to stay between the two characters.
  • Because of a lack of time during this project, characters are automatically assigned to each player controller.
  • A single blueprint serves as a parent for each different character. It stores all shared code, such as basic movement and attacks.

Characters' actions

  • Each move and attack has a frame data composed of Startup, Active and Recovery frames.
  • Attacks has their unique pushback, stun and damage values and spawns a custom hitbox during the active frames.
  • Going backwards will block incoming attacks, preventing them to deal damage. Blocking while standing only block normal and overhead attacks while crouch-blocking blocks normal and low attacks.
  • Alongside their attacks, each character has their own unique super attack and a grab, explained in the section below.

Attack system

  • Each character has its own set of attacks, including a light, heavy and special attack combined with a left joystick input.
  • If a player pushes this joystick up, their character will jump which allow air attacks. Moving forward while attacking can lead to different results than attacking without moving.
  • Doing or receiving damage will increase the Hype Meter, and using the "super" input when it's full will empty the meter and make a unique attack.
  • Characters can also grab their opponent, damaging and preventing them to attack or move for a short period of time.
  • Each attack has a frame data manually written in the code, according to the system documents and animations.

Round system

  • Starting a fight will start a round. In the first one, each player's character has 2 lifes. Loosing a round will remove one life and start the next one. The first player to remove all of their opponent's lives wins.
  • A timer of 99 seconds runs down during each round. When it reaches 0, the character with the less amount of health looses a life.
