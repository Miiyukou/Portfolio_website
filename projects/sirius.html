Sirius

  • Genre: Action-Adventure
  • Mode: Singleplayer
  • Development Time: 5 weeks
  • Team: 9 people
  • Platform: PC

Sirius was the first end-of-year project after our preparatory year. It was a safe project with no big ambition, with simple platforming mechanics and a simple AI.
Looking back at it, the game should've been less slow and would need more feedbacks, but it was a nice start.

Character's actions

  • The character can jump and double jump to reach some platforms.
  • They can also attack and block. Using the block action just before taking damage from an enemy makes a parry, which stuns the enemy.
  • Aside from these specific features, movement speed and jump height were important for a good feeling of platforming.
  • A bit after the start of the level, the character get a tool that extends their actions. It is explained in its own part below.

Enemy AI

  • Made with a behaviour tree, the enemy checks its distance from the character. If they are far from each other, it will charge towards the character, or else it attacks.
  • If the enemy misses the character and hit a wall after charging, it will be stunned.
  • The enemy has also a stun state if the player successfully parried one of its attacks.

Character's tool

  • During the level, the character gets a tool with two modes: grappling hook and magnet. It can be changed with an input.
  • Both can be aimed to be used more precisely, highlighting elements that can be interacting with it.
  • The Grappling Hook moves the character to where they aimed. The Magnet do the opposite: it brings an element towards the character.
