Mr.Chaussette Battle Tournament

  • Genre: Beat'em Up
  • Mode: 2-player
  • Development Time: 4 months
  • Team: 15 people
  • Platform: PC

This project was made as the final year project. We decided to change the original idea of Mr.Chaussette Battle Tournament to now be a Beat'em Up so the project could evolve and easily add new features.
We used the Versus Fighting project to redo and improve the multiplayer part, the camera and the attack system.

Characters' movement

  • To simulate the movements of a classic Beat'em up game, we decided to constrain the movement to left and right only for the 2D part. The up and down directions are only added later in the level.
  • Jumping works the same as most Beat'em Up and Fighting Games ; there is no air control. The direction of a jump is decided with a directional input.
  • Characters always face the camera, especially the 2D character, in order to avoid any visual oddities.
  • As the Vertical Slice ends with a fight between the two characters, everything was designed so that the Beat'em Up and the Fighting Game parts remain similar.

Attack system

  • Each character has their own set of attacks, which are always a 3-hit combo, a special attack, an air attack and a special air attack.
  • The special attacks are always different for each character: the 2D character has a dash that deals damage at the end of the movement and a projectile launched vertically in front of her for her air special. The 3D character's ground special attack makes him jump, while the air special launch a projectile in a diagonal line.
  • All attacks follow the same order of Startup, Active and Recovery frames based on the animation.
  • There is also a cancel attack system, allowing some attacks to end sooner to combo with a new one ; the 3-hit combo works with this system, and each of these 3 attacks can be canceled into the ground special attack.

Animation Integration

  • As the project has both a 3D and a 2D character, two separate methods has been used :
    • The 3D character has an animation blueprint, which follows the actual movement state of the character (Idle, Walking, Jumping) as well as the current attack.
    • Since the 2D character cannot have an animation blueprint because there is no skeleton, I worked with a 2D Paper Flipbook. A function was made to select the correct flipbook to play for each state like the other character.

Enemy AI

  • All enemies follows a simple logic ; they move to a random character and then they attack them. The target changes each time they lose their target before attacking.
  • Each enemy has their own health and speed, depending of their archetype (Light, Fast, and Heavy).
  • The heavy enemy takes reduced damage when attacked from the font. To deal a normal amount of damage, characters have to strike in their back.

Feedback

  • Feedback was the most challenging part to do, but also one of the more important.
  • To achieve good feedback, some points were important :
    • Hitting an enemy causes hitstop – it freezes the character and this enemy to give a feeling of power. Enemies hit turn red and get in their hit animation.
    • A VFX spawns at the location where the enemy was hit.
    • Hitstop and a hit animation also happen when a character is hit.

QA and Debugging

  • During playtests, a lot of bugs were reported on Mantis BT and were treated by priority.
  • A lot of these bugs affecting the game's quality were simple errors, quickly resolvable.
  • Feedback was the source of a lot of bugs, especially the hitstop. It was sometimes set multiple times because of a combo and so the character could not move for seconds. To solve this, hitstop was only set to certain attacks.
